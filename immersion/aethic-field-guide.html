Part I: The Aethic Definitional Hierarchy

Before examining the core principles, we must understand the mathematical foundation upon which Aethic reasoning is built: the Aethus and its hierarchical construction.

1.1 The Concept of an Aethus

The Aethus (plural: Aethae) is the central mathematical object of Aethic reasoning. It represents the information-anchored experiential locus of an observer—not a spacetime point, but a metaphysical coordinate on a Markov chain of block universes.

Key properties distinguishing the Aethus from conventional “informational states”:

  1. Algebraically structured: Capable of undergoing operations (union, intersection) that are ontologically prior to probabilistic or thermodynamic considerations
  2. Not Shannon-theoretic: Defined not by entropic constraints but by its role as a metaphysical coordinate
  3. Derivational: Serves as the primary object from which an observer’s entire block universe—their realized reality—is derived

1.2 Hierarchical Construction (1-Aethus through 4-Aethus)

The Aethus is constructed through a formal progression of increasingly abstract structures:

Level 0: States and Attributes

  • State: A single piece of raw data (e.g., the integer ‘8’)
  • Attribute: A class serving as a specific, signified question about reality (e.g., “the Mohs hardness of topaz”)
    • Contains: identifier, class itself, and a relation set encoding dependencies on other attributes

Level 1: The 1-Aethus (Stated-Attribute)

The fundamental unit of fact, formed by instantiating an Attribute with a specific State.

Example: (Mohs hardness, 8)

Conceptually: If an Attribute is like a question, a Stated-Attribute is like an answer.

Special case: The blank state represents unknowing—a stated-attribute that does not possess a definite state for its attribute.

Level 2: The 2-Aethus (Static Aethus)

A specific set of Stated-Attributes describing a system, viewed as a function mapping Attributes to States.

Example:

{
  ("Is harder than quartz?", Boolean): True,
  ("Contains silicon?", Boolean): True
}

Contains only explicitly listed facts.

Level 3: The 3-Aethus (Logical Closure)

The complete logical closure of a 2-Aethus—the exhaustive set of all derivable facts including:

  • All decompositions
  • All combinations
  • All congruent stated-attributes

While informationally complete, a 3-Aethus is mathematically unwieldy and infinitely redundant.

Level 4: The 4-Aethus (The Aethus Proper)

The equivalence class of all 2-Aethae generating the identical 3-Aethus. Just as “10”, “010.0”, and “9.99…” represent the same number, different Static Aethae can represent the same total knowledge.

Formal Definition: An Aethus is an equivalence class of lists of properties describing a system which may all derive one another in full.

Innovation

  3. Observer-relativity of reality (Wigner’s Friend scenarios)

Structural Facet

The extrusion principle recognizes that neither the growing block model nor the static block universe model is fully adequate:

  • Static block universe: Consistent with special relativity but cannot account for experienced time flow or quantum collapse
  • Growing block universe: Accounts for time flow but conflicts with relativity (requires privileged “now”)

Solution: Assert the validity of the static block universe model while allowing an extra effective direction out from the block universe—points along a larger structure of many block universes in sequence.

This is the Markov chain structure: reality is not a single block, but a progression along a Markov chain of block universes. Each node is a complete, static, 4D spacetime consistent with relativity.

Qualitative Facet

An observer’s subjective experience of time’s passage is their movement along this chain, driven by the acquisition of new information.

Core Reframe: The particle never changed—your Aethus did.

Instead of collapse occurring within a single timeline, the observer’s Aethus transitions to a new block universe where the outcome was always a fact.

Consider a sign on the side of the road: you observe it on the left. According to the Extrusion Principle, both outcomes—sign on left and sign on right—should coexist as a physical ‘and’ in your block universe. Yet you only observe one. Why don’t we see the sign on both sides at once?

4.2 The Resolution

Union Principle: Classical physics conflates logical structure with physical presence, treating them as synonymous. Aethic reasoning separates these domains: while classical logic remains valid for abstract reasoning, physical reality manifests both logical disjunctions and conjunctions as occupation of the block universe—what we perceive as physical ‘and’. The apparent classical ‘or’ (mutual exclusivity) emerges not from fundamental physics but from nested logical structure that forbids interaction between states while allowing their simultaneous presence.

Key Innovation

Logical ‘or’ realizes as physical ‘and’, satisfying the “two places at once” requirement, while still preserving non-contradiction by ensuring that disjoint attributes, when taken in intersection, form an “Aethic contradiction” (invalid Aethus).

5.2 The Delayed-Choice Experiment: A Deep Treatment

Wheeler’s delayed-choice experiment poses perhaps the most philosophically challenging question in quantum foundations: how can a particle “decide” to behave as a wave or particle before we’ve decided to measure it? The experiment seems to require either backward causation or an abandonment of any coherent temporal ontology. Aethic reasoning provides not merely a resolution, but uses this experiment to uniquely vindicate the Extrusion Principle itself.

5.2.1 The Paradox in Classical and Copenhagen Framings

In the standard delayed-choice setup:

  1. A photon enters an interferometer (or double-slit apparatus)
  2. At some point after the photon has “already passed through” the slits, the experimenter decides whether to insert a detector (which-path measurement) or not
  3. The measurement outcome—interference pattern or particle-like distribution—correlates perfectly with the later choice

The Copenhagen Problem: If the wavefunction collapses upon measurement, and measurement occurs after the photon has traversed the apparatus, then the collapse seems to reach backward in time to determine whether the photon went through one slit or both. This apparent retrocausality has no mechanism in Copenhagen.

The Timeline Paradox: In a timeline-branching model (including naive Many-Worlds), the question becomes deeply confused: if at time A the which-path information clearly exists, but at later time B it has been erased, what is ontologically occurring at A? Does the future erasure “jump back” and modify the past? Does time B spawn a new timeline where the agent at A becomes a philosophical zombie?

5.2.2 The Extrusion Principle as Unique Resolution

The extrusion principle dissolves this paradox with striking elegance by eliminating the assumption of a singular, growing timeline:

Core Resolution: The problem is with the timeline paradigm itself. By replacing timeline-branching with the block universe Markov chain structure, we directly address the intrinsic paradigmatic issues of retrocausality.

Mechanism: 1. Before decision: Observer occupies a block universe containing a disagreeing superposition of all possible measurement choices 2. After decision: Observer extrudes to a block universe where the coexisting past matches their informational update

12.2 The May 2023 Breakthrough

The final piece—the precise formulation of the Third Postulate—came during the week before final exams in freshman year of college. As Benander recalls:

“I knew the existing inheritance statement had to be true, but I also noted that it was going to have to be true for not only the observer’s Aethus in the Aethic soccer field, but for every possible one of their child Aethae. 12.4 The Framework’s Character

Several features of Aethic reasoning reflect its development by a young mind unconstrained by entrenched interpretive commitments:

  • Willingness to question fundamentals: The framework challenges not just quantum interpretation but basic assumptions about logical structure (Dual Logic Principle, Union Principle)
  • Thought experiment-driven: Heavy reliance on concrete, visualizable scenarios rather than pure formalism
  • Integration across domains: Draws connections to Eastern philosophy, epistemology, and anthropic reasoning that specialists might overlook
  • Derivational rather than postulational: Emphasis on deriving results from minimal assumptions rather than adding axioms to explain phenomena

As Benander notes regarding the derivational approach: “We need only one instance of experimental invalidation to falsify the entire framework, but I have my hopes, on account of the simplicity of the postulates, that such may never come to pass after all. We just might have to be a little patient in order for the experiments to truly catch up with the theory.”

Conclusion

Aethic reasoning represents a genuine conceptual advance in quantum foundations—perhaps the most ambitious and internally coherent attempt in decades to solve the measurement problem while respecting both empirical constraints and philosophical rigor.

Whether ultimately correct or not, the framework offers:

  • A precise mathematical structure (the Aethus hierarchy)
  • Clear derivational pathways (Three Postulates → experimental predictions)
  • Novel resolutions to long-standing paradoxes
  • Methodological transparency following successful historical precedents
  • Extreme falsifiability—a single counterexample invalidates the entire framework

The central insight may be captured in a single phrase: The universe is a machine that works tirelessly to avoid contradictions.

If this is correct, Aethic reasoning has found a window into the deep logical structure of existence itself—revealing that quantum behavior is not arbitrary physical law, but the direct consequence of a cosmos committed, above all else, to logical consistency.
